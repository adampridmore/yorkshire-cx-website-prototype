Your First Race — A Practical Guide

Everything you need to know before race day, so you can focus on enjoying it.

Entering a race

All YCCA races require online pre-registration — you cannot enter on the day. Entry costs £12 for adults and £4–£6 for juniors depending on age category. See the Current Season page for the full fee breakdown.

Sign up via the Current Season page. Entries usually open 2–3 weeks before each race and close the evening before.

You do not need a racing licence for the Novice category — just enter and turn up.

What to bring

🚲

Your bike

Any bike is fine for a first race. Check the brakes work and tyres are inflated.

⛔

A helmet

Compulsory. Must be worn at all times on the course, including warm-up laps.

👔

Cycling kit

Padded shorts help. Wear layers you don't mind getting muddy — you will get muddy.

💧

Water & a snack

The race is under an hour, but have something for after.

🛌

Cash for parking

Some venues charge £3 for parking. Check the event page for details.

💋

Spare dry kit

Leave a full change of clothes in the car. You'll want them after.

What happens on the day

  1. Arrive & sign on

    Sign-on opens 90 minutes before your race. Find the sign-on tent, give your name, collect your race number and timing chip. Pin the number to your jersey or shorts.

  2. Walk or ride the course

    The course is open for pre-ride before the race starts. Walk or ride it slowly — look at the obstacles, the off-camber sections, where to dismount. One walk-through is enough.

  3. Warm up & get to the start

    A 10–15 minute gentle warm-up ride will help. Get to the start area 5 minutes before your race time. Novices typically start from the back — that is fine, it keeps the start safe.

  4. Race

    The hooter sounds and you're off. Race for as many laps as the time allows. You will likely be lapped — pull slightly right when a faster rider comes through, then carry on. Marshals will pull you when your time is up.

  5. Finish & results

    Ride through the finish area. Results are usually published within an hour. Return your timing chip at the finish area if required.

  6. Wash your bike

    Give your bike a wash when you get home — mud dries hard.

Common questions

I'm not very fit — will I be able to finish?

Yes. Cyclo-cross categories are time-based, not lap-based. You race until the time runs out, whatever pace that is. Novice and beginner categories are specifically for people new to the sport. Finishing a race feels great at any pace.

What if I get lapped?

Getting lapped is completely normal in your first few races. Experienced riders will call out as they approach. Pull slightly to the right, let them pass, and carry on. There is no shame in it — everyone starts here.

Do I need to dismount for the obstacles?

The course includes barriers you generally need to dismount and carry your bike over. Most courses also have steep run-ups where carrying is faster than riding. Marshals will be nearby. Watch what others do on your course walk-through.

What if it's really muddy?

Mud is a feature, not a bug — cyclo-cross was invented for exactly this. Lower your tyre pressure slightly (around 30–35 psi for a road bike, less for wider tyres). Ride through mud rather than around it. Bring a towel.

Do I need a racing licence?

No — not for the Novice or Newcomer categories. You can enter and race without a British Cycling licence. If you continue racing and want to ride at higher categories, a day licence (around £5) or annual licence becomes useful.
