Junior & Youth Riders

Cyclo-cross is one of the best sports for young riders — short, safe, exciting, and great fun in all weathers.

Why cyclo-cross for young riders?

🎯

Skill development

Balancing, dismounting, remounting, carrying a bike — CX builds bike-handling skills no road race can.

⏱

Short races

Junior races are 20–45 minutes depending on age. Exciting enough to engage young riders, short enough to leave them wanting more.

👥

Age-matched competition

Separate categories for every age group mean young riders race against peers, not adults.

🌞

All-weather adventure

Mud, grass and obstacles make CX genuinely exciting for kids who love being outdoors.

For parents

Does my child need a racing licence?

Under 12s can race without a licence at YCCA events. For Under 14 and above, a British Cycling day licence (around £5) is recommended. Annual junior memberships from British Cycling start at around £25 and include racing and insurance benefits.

What equipment does my child need?

A helmet (compulsory), any bike, and suitable clothing for the weather. Mountain bikes work well for juniors as they're robust and handle mud well. You don't need to buy a dedicated cyclo-cross bike to get started.

Do parents need to stay at the venue?

Yes — a parent or responsible adult must remain at the event for all junior riders (Under 18). You don't need to be trackside for the whole race, but you should be on site and reachable.

Is cyclo-cross safe for children?

Cyclo-cross is one of the safer cycling disciplines — speeds are low, courses are short-circuit and well-marshalled, and age-graded categories keep young riders racing with peers. Courses are designed to be challenging but not dangerous.

How do I enter my child in a race?

Visit the Current Season page for entry links. Pre-registration is required — you cannot enter on the day. Entry costs £4–£6 per race depending on age category.
